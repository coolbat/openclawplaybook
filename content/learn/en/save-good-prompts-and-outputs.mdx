---
title: Save Good Prompts and Good Outputs
summary: Treat prompt/output pairs as working assets so later workflow design becomes easier.
readingTime: 6 min
---

## Do not trust memory

When something works, save it. Good prompt structures and good outputs are not just examples. They are reusable building blocks for later workflows.

## What to keep

- prompt patterns that consistently frame the task well
- outputs that show the right level of detail
- failure examples that reveal bad assumptions

## Why this matters

Saved examples reduce future guesswork. They also make it easier to explain the workflow to teammates.
