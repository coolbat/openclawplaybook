## Bilingual does not mean duplicated

The goal of bilingual collaboration is shared understanding, not mirrored wording. Teams move faster when they align on concepts, handoff points, and output structure, even if phrasing differs across languages.

## Helpful habits

- keep workflow names stable across languages
- translate outcomes, not every internal note
- keep key decisions and exceptions explicit
- reuse output templates when possible

## Where friction appears

Friction usually comes from mixed vocabulary, unclear owners, or outputs that assume one language context but land in another.

> **Aim for equivalence**
>   Two versions should help the same decision happen, even if they are not
> sentence-by-sentence copies.
