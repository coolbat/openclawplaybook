## Instability has layers

Browser automation failures often look random, but they usually come from one of three places: environment instability, fragile selectors, or tasks that are too stateful.

## Diagnose in that order

- Is the runtime stable?
- Is the target page changing unexpectedly?
- Is the task waiting on UI states that are not explicit?

## Reduce fragility

Use smaller tasks, stronger selectors, and fewer hidden assumptions. If a workflow depends on long, stateful browser sessions, it will be harder to trust.

> **A better beginner move**
>   Prove one short browser action first. Then add the second step only after the
> first is stable.
