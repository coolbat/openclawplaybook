## Automation is not the same as delegation

The most common beginner mistake is assuming that once a workflow runs, it should also be trusted end to end. In practice, many workflows still need a human at the point of release, approval, or interpretation.

## Good places for human review

- Final publishing decisions
- Customer-facing replies
- Alerts that may trigger operational action
- Summaries that could omit critical context

## What OpenClaw should do first

Let the system reduce repetition:

- collect inputs
- structure messy information
- draft the first pass
- highlight what needs attention

Then keep the final judgment with a person until the workflow has earned trust over time.

> **A safer beginner pattern**
>   Use the system to prepare decisions before you use it to make decisions.
