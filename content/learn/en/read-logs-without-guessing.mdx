## Read logs in layers

Logs become useful when you stop treating them like a wall of text. Start from the visible symptom, then identify the failing stage, then read the environment around that failure.

## A practical order

1. What was the user-visible symptom?
2. Which workflow step likely failed?
3. What happened immediately before the error?
4. What changed recently?

## Avoid this trap

Do not rewrite config first and then read the log later. By then you may have destroyed the original evidence.

> **Logs are not proof alone**
>   Pair the log with the observed symptom. A scary message that had no user impact
> is not always your real problem.
