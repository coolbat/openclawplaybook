---
title: How to Choose Safer Starter Skills
summary: Start with narrow, inspectable skills before you hand the system anything high impact.
readingTime: 9 min
---

## Narrow skills teach faster

A good first skill does not need to be powerful. It needs to be understandable. If you can see what it receives, what it changes, and what it returns, you can learn faster and debug faster.

## Good starter traits

- small input surface
- visible output
- low operational risk
- easy manual review

## Bad first choices

Avoid workflows that combine many side effects at once, especially if they touch production systems, customer channels, or browser automation before you understand the system behavior.

> **Safer first move**
>   Prefer a skill that drafts, summarizes, or classifies before a skill that
> modifies, sends, or triggers.
