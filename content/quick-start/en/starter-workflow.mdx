Your first workflow should prove that OpenClaw can produce a result you can inspect quickly.

## Good first-workflow traits

The best starter workflows are:

- short feedback loop
- obvious output
- easy to rerun
- narrow enough to debug

Examples include:

- daily briefings
- summaries
- structured notes
- simple dispatch tasks

## What you are really proving

You are not trying to build the final system yet. You are proving that:

- the trigger arrives
- the logic runs
- the output lands somewhere useful

> “Useful and repeatable” matters more than “impressive.”

## Common beginner mistake

Starting with a workflow that is too broad creates ambiguous failure. When everything is coupled, nothing is easy to fix.

## A better next move

Once one starter workflow runs, you can either deepen your foundations in Learn or expand with Templates that match your goal.
