---
title: Workflow is too broad to debug
summary: You know something in the chain is wrong, but there are too many moving parts to isolate it.
readingTime: 4 min
---

## What this symptom usually means

The workflow has too many steps to debug as one unit. Broad flows hide the first real breakage and make every symptom look ambiguous.

## First recovery loop

Split the workflow into smaller checkpoints and inspect the first missing or degraded output. The goal is to reduce the problem to one failing step.

## What to verify next

Check whether the missing output is due to channel handling, model behavior, or the workflow structure itself. Those are different failure classes.

## If the issue persists

Compare the failing flow against a proven starter template. That gives you a stable baseline and reduces the number of unknowns.
